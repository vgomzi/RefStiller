Quick Info and Help

  • IMPORTANT!
    In definition of in and out formats, for elements of
      nm (abbreviated author name) and
      jd (abbreviated journal name),
    please DO NOT include '.' (dot) separator for in-record punctuation, as these are already taken care of.
    All other separator fields should be used at their appropriate places.

  • Available separators and descriptors for each of three acceptable sources journals, books and web sources are listed below.
  • Separators (all sources):
      [  ]  (  )  "  '  ,  .  ;
    Styles (all sources):
      <b> </b>   bold,    <u></u>   underline,    <i><i>   italic
    Descriptors - journals
    • r - reference number
    • nm - author's abbreviated name, or in list of names
    • name - author's full name, or in list of names
    • sur - author's surname, or in list of names
    • e - add 'et al.' in the author list
    • jd - journal name abbreviation in format "j. math. chem. comp."
    • journal - full journal name
    • y - year
    • title - article title
    • vol - volume
    • nu - number
    • p - page
    • pp - pages in format: "from-to"
    Descriptors - books
    • r - reference number
    • nm - author's abbreviated name, or in list of names
    • name - author's full name, or in list of names
    • sur - author's surname, or in list of names
    • e - add 'et al.' in the author list
    • b - book
    • y - year
    • title - title
    • pub - publisher
    • nu - number
    • p - page
    • pp - pages in format: "from-to"
    Descriptors - web sources
    • r - reference number
    • nm - author's abbreviated name, or in list of names
    • name - author's full name, or in list of names
    • sur - author's surname, or in list of names
    • e - add 'et al.' in the author list
    • w - URL of website
    • y - year
    • title - title
    • d - date (freeformat, not really parsed, just copied)
    • a - include word 'accessed'
